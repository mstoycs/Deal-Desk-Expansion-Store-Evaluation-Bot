About Expansion Store Evaluation

Overview

Eddie the Expansion Store Evaluator is designed to automatically assess whether a requested store qualifies as an expansion store under an existing main shop URL. This evaluation is based on the criteria defined in Section 9.13 of the Plus Agreement.

Evaluation Criteria

Online Stores (Clones)

To qualify as an expansion store, online stores must meet ALL of the following criteria:

  • Brand Extension: Must be extensions of the Main Brand
  • Branding Identity: Must be identical to the Main Brand with respect to Store name and other branding
  • Product Identity: Must carry the identical goods and services as the Main Brand
  • Product Names: Must have at least 3 identically named products as the Main Store
  • Business Type Compatibility: D2C main stores can have one B2B expansion store, and B2B main stores can have one D2C expansion store
  • B2B Qualification: B2B expansion stores must require login for cart functionality and pricing visibility
  • Language/Currency: May differ in language and applicable currency (this is allowed)
Physical Retail & Wholesale Stores

Physical retail locations or wholesale stores must meet the following criteria:

  • Branding Identity: Must be identical to the Main Brand with respect to Store name and other branding
  • Product Types: Must carry the same types of goods and services as the Main Brand
Development & Staging Stores

Development and staging stores have special considerations:

  • Must meet pre-approved expansion store criteria
  • These stores are free and unlimited until moved to production
  • Once moved to production, they are priced according to expansion store rules
High Store Volume (Agency) Stores

Important note about high volume stores:

  • Expansion store rules do NOT apply to High Store Volume pricing frameworks
  • These agreements are priced by the total number of concurrent stores
  • Different evaluation criteria apply
D2C/B2B Business Type Compatibility

Eddie now supports evaluation of different business models:

  • D2C Main Store → B2B Expansion Store: A merchant with a D2C main store is permitted one B2B expansion store provided it closely aligns in branding, products, and themes
  • B2B Main Store → D2C Expansion Store: A B2B main store is permitted to have one qualified D2C expansion store provided it is closely aligned to branding, products, and themes
  • B2B Qualification: B2B expansion stores must require login for cart functionality and pricing visibility to qualify

How It Works

The evaluation bot analyzes the following aspects of the requested store:

  • URL Analysis: Extracts store name, branding elements, language, and currency from the URL
  • Store Type Detection: Identifies whether it's an online, physical, wholesale, development, or staging store
  • Brand Comparison: Compares branding elements with the main store
  • Product Analysis: Analyzes goods and services (requires web scraping in production)
  • Criteria Assessment: Evaluates against the appropriate criteria based on store type

Confidence Scoring

The bot provides a confidence score based on the quality and completeness of the analysis:

  • High Confidence (80%+): Complete data available for all criteria
  • Medium Confidence (50-79%): Partial data available, some criteria may be estimated
  • Low Confidence (<50%): Limited data available, manual review recommended
Quick Reference
Qualified Expansion Store:

✓ Meets ALL required criteria for its store type

Unqualified Expansion Store:

✗ Does not meet all required criteria

High Volume Stores:

⚠ Different pricing framework applies
